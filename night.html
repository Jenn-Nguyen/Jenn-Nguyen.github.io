Night Photography

 

Background Info

My first successful night shots of the Melbourne City. This is was New Year’s Eve 2019. My family decided to go see the fireworks, something we do every year for fun. I wanted to utilise this moment to practice night photography and capture some fireworks. That didn’t go as planned, instead I got photos of the city instead.

How

A tripod would be recommended when doing night photography. It helps to stabilise the camera. I didn’t have a tripod on hand, instead I placed my camera down on the railing of a bridge. I used the manual setting on a DSLR.

  • Shutter speed/exposure = 3.2”
  • Aperture = f/29
  • ISO = 1600

As the place was extremely dark, the shutter speed value was higher, 3.2”. This allowed the camera to be exposed to the light a little longer. The aperture was set to f/29, making the image more focus. Less light will be captured with a high aperture. However setting the ISO to 1600 made the overall image a bit brighter.
